FIVE SECTIONS. EVERY REPORT.

The brief, structured.

Every generated report follows the same five-section structure, so you never have to hunt for the information you need. The structure is deliberately designed to match how experienced property managers think about their operation — top-down, from the overall health of the property to the specific steps you should take today.

1. Executive Summary — two or three sentences describing the current state of the property. What's going well, what needs attention. Suitable for copy-pasting into an owner email.

2. Priority Action Items — a numbered list of the top 5 most urgent things to do right now, pulled from your overdue tasks, unread messages, and upcoming lease events. Each item is specific: includes resident names, unit numbers, amounts, and dates.

3. AI Recommendations — four to five strategic suggestions that go beyond your current task list. Market insights, workflow improvements, financial optimizations — the kind of thinking a thoughtful consultant would provide if they had time to study your data.

4. Activity Insights — analysis of recent patterns: communication frequency, response times, task completion pace. Surfaces things you'd never notice manually.

5. This Week's Focus — three concrete things the AI recommends you focus on in the next seven days. Specific, motivating, action-oriented.

LIVE DATA, NOT TEMPLATES

Every report is custom-built, every time.

Generic templates produce generic reports. A template-based "weekly update" might say "The property is doing well this week" regardless of whether rent collection is at 100% or 60%. That's useless, and property owners know it the moment they read one.

Modern Management's reports are genuinely custom. The AI reads your actual live data at the moment you click Generate — every unread message, every open task, every overdue rent record, every upcoming lease expiration, every maintenance ticket, every budget transaction for the current month. It then writes a report that reflects your property's specific situation, referencing your actual residents by name and your actual numbers.

If the property has a problem this week, the report surfaces it. If everything's running smoothly, the report says so — and uses the quiet time to recommend strategic moves (proactive maintenance, renewal outreach, market rate adjustments). Reports aren't noise; they're signal.
